# 01 — Content Strategy
> What gets made, why, and how often. Every piece serves the 3-Blueprint-clients goal.

## The five pillars

| # | Pillar | Job | Formats | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Authority** | Name the problem precisely. "This person has seen this before." | Carousel (naming the gap), single post (POV), reel (opinion, no CTA) | Every week — non-negotiable. The foundation. |
| 02 | **Education** | Teach the framework. "I didn't know this had a name." | Carousel (step-by-step), reel (concept fast), save-bait posts | Every week. Alternates with Authority. |
| 03 | **Process** | Studio in action. "They actually do this." | Reel (B-roll + text overlay), story, desk-detail post | Week 2 onwards. |
| 04 | **Offer** | Direct. Specific. Reader knows exactly what to do. | Carousel (offer breakdown), reel (what you get), announcement | Every other week; more often during beta. |
| 05 | **Social Proof** | Results over adjectives. Anonymised until explicit permission. | Reel (client story), before/after carousel, story | When available. **Never fabricate.** |

## Minimum viable cadence (v1 — do not exceed until it's a habit)

**3 posts per week:**
- Slot A: Authority carousel or statement post
- Slot B: Education carousel or reel
- Slot C: rotates — Offer (weeks 1, 3) / Process (weeks 2, 4)
- Vera videos slot into B or C when a script is approved — max 1/week
- Stories: opportunistic, zero production overhead, no planning required

Consistency at 3/week beats bursts at 7/week. Launch at 70%.

## The six content principles — pre-publish gate

Check every piece against all six. Fails more than one → rewrite.

1. **Name a tension the ICP already feels.** A familiar problem finally articulated with precision — recognition in the first three seconds.
2. **Communicate without convincing.** Velum states; the right client self-selects. Persuasion language signals the wrong category.
3. **Outcomes over vibes.** Reference a concrete change — offer architecture, monetisation plan, 30-day sequence.
4. **Earn the scroll in the first line.** Hooks are the problem stated precisely — not clever openings.
5. **One CTA. Named. Specific.**
6. **Faceless — always.**

## Hook doctrine

A hook is the ICP's exact situation, stated as fact, with no cushioning.

**Working examples (validated register):**
- "Most creators don't have a business. They have a hobby with a following."
- "Your offer isn't the problem. Your offer architecture is."
- "One service is a gig. A ladder is a business."
- "Coaches give advice. Operators build the thing."

**Structure patterns that work:** the reframe (X isn't the problem, X-architecture is) · the distinction (gig vs. business, coach vs. operator) · the uncomfortable census (posting for months, growing audience, no offer) · the precise number ("40K followers and €0 in structured revenue").

## Topic sourcing — inputs to the machine

Priority order:
1. **Real client/discovery-call signals** — a question, objection, or gap surfaced this week (anonymised)
2. **Blueprint framework components** — offer ladder, maturity levels, monetisation roadmap
3. **Operator observations** — patterns seen across the ICP via Emplifi prospecting
4. **The build itself** — Process pillar content

One real signal per week is enough to feed all three slots via the repurposing agent.
