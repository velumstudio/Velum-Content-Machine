## The Problem We Solve

The gap between an audience and a business is structural, not creative. Solving it requires architecture — offer design, monetisation logic, sequencing — not more content or better aesthetics.

**Surface symptom:** "I post consistently and people love what I make. I'm not making real money from it."

**The real problem:** No offer architecture. She's monetising by accident — brand deals, one-off products, a course she launched once. Revenue is inconsistent, underpriced, and structurally disconnected from her actual audience size and trust level.

### The three gap types

1. **No structure** — No offer visible anywhere. Link in bio: affiliate links only. Monetisation = brand deals. Nothing owned.
2. **Thin structure** — One product or service, inconsistently promoted. Pricing feels uncertain. No clear entry point or offer ladder.
3. **Misaligned structure** — Has launched something. It didn't land. Offer doesn't match her audience or content positioning. Exhausted from trying without a system.

### Outcome layers

| Layer | Definition |
|---|---|
| Functional | A documented offer architecture and 30-day plan she can act on immediately, without further input. |
| Emotional | "This finally makes sense." Commercial clarity after months — sometimes years — of creative success without business structure. |
| Identity | She is no longer a creator trying to figure out the business side. She is a business with a creative front. |

## Offer Ladder

### 0 — Monetisation Audit Quiz (Free · Lead gen)
3-minute self-qualification. 8 questions. 4 maturity levels. Drives segmented email nurture. Primary top-of-funnel mechanism.

**The four maturity levels:**
1. The Creative — No commercial structure. Pure creative output.
2. The Experimenter — Has tried something. It didn't compound.
3. The Builder — Has structure. Needs sequencing and architecture.
4. The Operator — Commercial clarity. Ready for deeper engagement.

### 1 — Launch Toolkit (€97–147 · Digital product)
Semi-custom async operational guide. 5-part recipe structure. Offered alongside Shadow Partnership at Blueprint delivery call close — the two paths forward once architecture exists.

**Deliverable structure:**
1. Diagnosis summary — the commercial gap, in her language
2. The prescription — 3–5 tools maximum, with one-line rationale each
3. 90-day sequence — install order, not a shopping list
4. Shadow-fit note — stated once, quietly, never as a pitch
5. Closing note — soft: what would change if Velum built this with her instead

### 2 — Velum Blueprint (€389 beta → €850 standard · PRIMARY OFFER)
Strategic sprint. 5–7 working days. ~5–6 hours operator time per client.

**Deliverables:**
- Structured async intake questionnaire
- Offer architecture — core structure mapped
- Monetisation positioning — what to lead with and why
- 30-day priority plan — sequenced, named actions
- 1 delivery call (60 min, recorded)
- Notion doc — clean, structured, client's to keep
- 1 round async follow-up (48h window)

**Gate:** Three validated Blueprint clients before anything else scales. This is governance, not preference.

### 3 — Quarterly Strategic Session (€250–300 · Blueprint graduates only)
90-minute strategic session. Course-correction or next-phase planning. Not publicly offered. Not available before first Blueprint client.

### 4 — Offer Build Sprint (€1,500–2,500 · Selective)
Operator builds the commercial layer with the client — not just maps it. Scoped per engagement.

### 5 — Shadow Partnership (30/70 revenue share · By invitation only)
**Never publicly named. Never publicly described.**

For a small number of Blueprint clients, the work doesn't stop there. Velum stays in the background — on a revenue-share basis — through the digital product launch and beyond.

- Offered at the end of the Blueprint delivery call, alongside Launch Toolkit
- 30/70 split — creator keeps 70%, Velum takes 30%, calculated on net after platform fees
- Selective — not every Blueprint client is invited
- The Blueprint builds the trust that makes this obvious
- Public hint language only: *"Some engagements go further — by invitation only."*

### Future (locked until 10 validated Blueprint clients)
Creator Business Dashboard — ~€29/month micro-SaaS. Do not build before that gate.

**Sounds like:** A senior strategist who has solved this problem before. An operator who thinks in systems, speaks in outcomes. An editor who cuts what doesn't need to be there.

**Tone dimensions:**
- Calm authority — never loud, never urgent

## Content Strategy

### The five content pillars

| # | Pillar | Purpose | Signal of success |
|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | Authority | Names the problem with precision. "This person has seen this before." | "This is exactly how I feel." |
| 02 | Education | Teaches the framework. Makes the commercial gap visible and legible. | "I didn't know this had a name." |
| 03 | Process | Studio in action. Faceless working moments. | "They actually do this." |
| 04 | Offer | Direct, specific, no softening. Reader knows exactly what to do. | DM 'BLUEPRINT' |
| 05 | Social Proof | Results over adjectives. Anonymised until explicit permission. | Before/after commercial gap |

### Content principles (non-negotiable)

1. Name a tension the ICP already feels — recognition in the first three seconds
2. Communicate without convincing — Velum states; the right client self-selects
3. Outcomes over vibes — reference concrete changes, not atmosphere
4. Earn the scroll in the first line — the hook IS the problem, stated precisely
5. One CTA per piece. Named. Specific.
6. Faceless always

### Hook doctrine

A hook is the ICP's exact situation, stated as fact, with no cushioning.

**Working hooks (validated register):**
- "Most creators don't have a business. They have a hobby with a following."
- "An audience is not a business. Most creators are still figuring out the difference."
- "Your offer isn't the problem. Your offer architecture is."
- "One service is a gig. A ladder is a business."
- "Coaches give advice. Operators build the thing."
- "She had 40K followers and €0 in structured revenue. That's a structure problem, not a talent problem."
- "An audience is attention. A business is structure."
