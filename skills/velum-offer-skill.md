## The three gap types — diagnostic framework

Every ICP falls into one of three commercial gaps. Identifying the right gap is the first job of the Blueprint.

### Gap 1 — No structure
No offer visible anywhere. Link in bio: affiliate links only. Monetisation = brand deals. Nothing owned.

**Signal:** "I don't really sell anything. I do brand deals."
**Diagnosis:** She has never built a commercial layer. Needs offer architecture from scratch.
**Blueprint entry point:** Offer design first, monetisation roadmap second.

### Gap 2 — Thin structure
One product or service, inconsistently promoted. Pricing feels uncertain. No clear entry point. No offer ladder.

**Signal:** "I have a course/service but it doesn't sell consistently."
**Diagnosis:** Something exists but isn't structured. Pricing, positioning, or sequencing is off.
**Blueprint entry point:** Offer audit first, architecture second.

### Gap 3 — Misaligned structure
Has launched something. It didn't land. Offer doesn't match audience trust or content positioning. Exhausted from trying.

**Signal:** "I launched something and it didn't work. I don't know why."
**Diagnosis:** The offer exists but is positioned wrong for the audience she's actually built.
**Blueprint entry point:** Positioning audit first, re-architecture second.

## Blueprint delivery document — structure

The Notion deliverable follows this exact sequence:

```
01 — COMMERCIAL DIAGNOSIS
What gap type she's in (1/2/3). Named precisely. No softening.

02 — AUDIENCE TRUST ASSESSMENT
What she's actually built: size, engagement depth, content trust level.
What the audience would buy if the right offer existed.

03 — OFFER ARCHITECTURE
The offer ladder designed for her specific audience:
  - Entry point: [name, price, format, what it delivers]
  - Mid-tier: [name, price, format, what it delivers]
  - Top offer: [name, price, format — only if trust depth supports it]

04 — MONETISATION POSITIONING
What to lead with and why. Sequencing rationale.
What not to lead with and why.

05 — 30-DAY PRIORITY PLAN
Named, sequenced actions. Not a list of ideas — a plan.
Actions have an order. The order matters.

06 — SHADOW-FIT NOTE (if applicable)
One sentence only. Quiet. Not a pitch.
```

## Offer diagnosis workflow — for client sessions

When reviewing a client's current offer situation:

1. **Categorise the gap** (1 / 2 / 3) from discovery call signals
2. **Assess audience trust** — what has she built, and does her current offer match it?
3. **Identify the entry point** — what's the lowest-friction offer she could make to this audience right now?
4. **Map the ladder** — what logically follows the entry point, and in what order?
5. **Flag the mismatch** — if her current price points don't match her trust depth, name it plainly
6. **Sequence the 30-day plan** — first action, second action, third action. No "eventually" items.

## Offer content for Instagram

When building offer-pillar content:

**Rule:** offer posts are direct, specific, and free of pitch energy. They describe the deliverable — not the transformation. The reader knows exactly what she gets and what to do next.

**What to include:**
- What it is (sprint, not coaching programme)
- What she walks away with (the documented output)
- Timeline (5–7 working days)
- The one CTA (`DM 'BLUEPRINT'`)
- Shadow hint if appropriate (closing slide only)

**What to exclude:**
- Testimonial language before social proof exists
- Price in most content — price lives in the DM conversation
- Outcome promises that exceed what the Blueprint delivers
- Any language that sounds like coaching, transformation, or community

**€389 beta:** validates the model, builds the first case studies, generates the social proof that makes €850 obvious. Every beta client is research. Do not discount beyond beta price.
