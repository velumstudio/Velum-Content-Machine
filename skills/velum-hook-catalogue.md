## What a hook is — and isn't

A hook is the ICP's exact situation, stated as fact, with no cushioning.
It is not a clever opener. It is not a question that flatters. It is not a motivational observation.

**The test:** Would she stop scrolling if she saw only this line — with no image, no context, no brand mark?
If yes — it's a hook. If no — it's a setup. Rewrite it.

**The second test:** Does it sound like something she has already thought, but never heard said out loud?
If yes — recognition. If no — keep rewriting.

## The 8 hook patterns

### Pattern 1 — The Distinction
Two things the ICP confuses. Named plainly. The second half lands harder than the first.

**Structure:** `[Thing A] is [category]. [Thing B] is [better category].`

**Why it works:** Creates an in-group/out-group split instantly. The reader self-identifies. The distinction feels obvious once named — which means she shares it to prove she already knew.

**Velum examples:**
- `One service is a gig. A ladder is a business.`
- `Coaches give advice. Operators build the thing.`
- `An audience is attention. A business is structure.`
- `Posting is visibility. An offer ladder is revenue.`
- `Brand deals are income. Digital products are assets.`

**Use for:** Authority, Education
**Strongest format:** Carousel cover, statement post, Vera opening line
**CTA pairing:** `Save this. Come back when you're ready to build.`

---

### Pattern 2 — The Uncomfortable Census
A count of a situation the ICP is in. States the reality without naming her directly. She recognises herself.

**Structure:** `[Situation]. [Situation]. [Gap they can't name].`

**Why it works:** Three short declarative sentences in sequence create rhythm. Each one lands. By the third, she's already identified. No question needed — she just said yes three times without being asked.

**Velum examples:**
- `Posting for months. Growing audience. Still no clear offer.`
- `12K followers. A course she launched once. Brand deals that don't compound.`
- `The content converts to followers. The followers don't convert to clients.`
- `She posts consistently. People love what she makes. She's not making real money.`
- `Real audience. Real trust. No commercial structure underneath it.`

**Use for:** Authority, Social Proof
**Strongest format:** Reel hook (text overlay), Vera opening sequence, carousel cover
**CTA pairing:** `DM 'BLUEPRINT' if this is where you are.`

---

### Pattern 3 — The Reframe
Names what the ICP thinks the problem is. Replaces it with the real problem. The real problem is structural.

**Structure:** `[Symptom] isn't the problem. [Root cause] is.`
*Or the positive version:* `[Thing she has] is not the problem. [Thing she's missing] is.`

**Why it works:** Validates her effort before redirecting it. She has been trying to solve the wrong thing — and this makes that feel obvious, not embarrassing. Relief, not blame.

**Velum examples:**
- `Your offer isn't the problem. Your offer architecture is.`
- `The content isn't the problem. The commercial layer underneath it is.`
- `She doesn't need more followers. She needs a structure that converts the ones she has.`
- `This isn't a visibility problem. It's an architecture problem.`
- `The audience is there. The offer ladder isn't.`

**Use for:** Education, Authority
**Strongest format:** Carousel cover, statement post
**CTA pairing:** `Save this. Come back when you're ready to build.` / `Link in bio.`

**⚠ Warning:** Do not use the negation formula ("This isn't X. It's not Y. It's Z." — three negations in sequence). One reframe is a hook. Three negations is a banned pattern. See 00-brand-constitution.md.

### Pattern 4 — The Precise Number
A specific data point from the ICP's world. The specificity is what creates credibility and shareability.

**Structure:** `[Specific metric]. [Gap or result].`

**Why it works:** Round numbers feel invented. Specific numbers feel witnessed. "40K followers" is more credible than "thousands of followers." The gap after the number creates tension that demands resolution.

**Velum examples:**
- `She had 40K followers and €0 in structured revenue. That's a structure problem, not a talent problem.`
- `40K followers. €0 in structured revenue. 30 days later: a real offer ladder.`
- `5–7 working days. A documented offer structure. A 30-day plan she can execute without further input.`
- `15 years of digital business. One thing matters more than everything else.`
- `Most creators are one offer away from revenue. They just don't know which offer.`

**Use for:** Social Proof, Offer, Authority
**Strongest format:** Reel hook (text overlay), carousel cover, Vera second beat
**CTA pairing:** `DM 'BLUEPRINT' to start yours.`

---

### Pattern 5 — The Identity Shift
Describes who she is now and who she becomes. The shift is the deliverable — not the output, the identity.

**Structure:** `She is no longer [old identity]. She is [new identity].`
*Or compressed:* `From [old state] to [new state]. That shift is the real deliverable.`

**Why it works:** This is the deepest recognition hook. It names what she actually wants — not revenue, not an offer, but to stop feeling like a creator trying to figure out the business side. That feeling is real and rarely named.

**Velum examples:**
- `She is no longer a creator trying to figure out the business side. She is a business with a creative front.`
- `Same content. Same niche. Same audience. The only thing that changed was the commercial layer underneath.`
- `You built the audience. Nobody taught you how to make a business out of it.`
- `Most creators don't have a business. They have a hobby with a following.`
- `She stopped monetising by accident. She started monetising by design.`

**Use for:** Social Proof, Authority
**Strongest format:** Closing slide of carousel, Vera closing line, caption body
**CTA pairing:** `DM 'BLUEPRINT' to start yours.`

---

### Pattern 6 — The Object Specific
Names a concrete object from the ICP's world. Not a concept — a thing she can picture. The specificity creates recognition faster than any abstraction.

**Structure:** `[Specific object from her life]. [What it signals about her situation].`

**Why it works:** Abstract problems are easy to ignore. A specific object forces a specific memory. "A link in bio with affiliate links" is not generic — it is her link in bio. She looks at it every day.

**Velum examples:**
- `A link in bio with affiliate links. A course she launched once. Brand deals that didn't compound. That's not a business. That's a collection of attempts.`
- `An abandoned Gumroad page. A Calendly nobody books. A Google Doc she calls her offer. She has the pieces. She doesn't have the architecture.`
- `A Notion doc she built herself. A pricing page she's not sure about. A DM she didn't know how to answer. The structure is almost there.`
- `She has the audience. She has the trust. She has a highlight reel full of content that converts to followers. She doesn't have an offer ladder.`

**Use for:** Authority, Education
**Strongest format:** Carousel body slide, caption opening, statement post
**CTA pairing:** `DM 'BLUEPRINT' if this is where you are.`

---

### Pattern 7 — The Cost of Time
Names what compounds in the wrong direction while she waits. Not urgency — observation. The gap gets wider, not smaller, without intervention.

**Structure:** `Every [time unit] without [structural thing] is a [compounding cost].`
*Or:* `[Positive thing] compounds. [Gap] compounds too — in the other direction.`

**Why it works:** Not a countdown, not manufactured scarcity. A structural observation. Trust compounds — she knows this. The commercial gap compounds too. Both things are true. Naming them together creates quiet urgency without pressure language.

**Velum examples:**
- `Every month without commercial structure is a month the gap between her audience and her revenue gets wider.`
- `The audience keeps growing either way. The commercial gap compounds too — in the other direction.`
- `Trust compounds. Point of view sharpens. The offer architecture doesn't build itself.`
- `She's been building for two years. The audience is real. The commercial layer was never built. That's two years of compounding trust with no structure underneath it.`
- `The right moment to build offer architecture is when the audience is already there. Not after the next launch.`

**Use for:** Education, Offer
**Strongest format:** Reel hook, caption body, closing carousel slide before CTA
**CTA pairing:** `Link in bio. The first conversation is a DM.`

---

### Pattern 8 — The Operator Contrast
Positions Velum against what the ICP has already tried. Not disparagement — precision. What she got before was advice. What this is, is different.

**Structure:** `[What others give]. [What operators build].`
*Or:* `[What she's tried]. [Why it didn't compound]. [What was actually missing].`

**Why it works:** The ICP has usually tried something before — a coach, a course, a mastermind. It didn't compound. This hook names exactly why without blaming her. The tool was wrong for the problem, not her for trying.

**Velum examples:**
- `Coaches give advice. Operators build the thing. Here's the difference.`
- `A coach tells you what to do. An operator builds the system so it exists when the engagement ends.`
- `You own the advice. You own the output. That distinction is the entire difference.`
- `Not mindset. Not motivation. Not accountability. Structural deliverables. Specific to your audience. Immediately executable.`
- `She's had the strategy conversation. She hasn't had someone build the system with her.`

**Use for:** Authority, Offer
**Strongest format:** Carousel cover, statement post, Vera second beat
**CTA pairing:** `Follow for the operator point of view.`

## Hook selection guide

**Stop the scroll — she's never heard it put this way:**
→ Pattern 1 (Distinction) or Pattern 6 (Object Specific)

**Save the post — she wants to come back to this:**
→ Pattern 3 (Reframe) or Pattern 7 (Cost of Time)

**Send a DM — she recognises herself completely:**
→ Pattern 2 (Uncomfortable Census) or Pattern 5 (Identity Shift)

**Follow the account — she trusts the perspective:**
→ Pattern 8 (Operator Contrast) or Pattern 4 (Precise Number)

**Vera video opening:**
→ Pattern 2 (Census as recognition questions) → Pattern 3 or 8 (declarative beats)

## Hook + pillar matrix

| Pillar | Best patterns | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Authority | 1 (Distinction), 2 (Census), 8 (Operator Contrast) | 5 (Identity Shift) — too emotional for Authority opener |
| Education | 3 (Reframe), 7 (Cost of Time), 6 (Object Specific) | 4 (Precise Number) alone — needs context |
| Process | 4 (Precise Number), 6 (Object Specific) | 8 (Operator Contrast) — wrong moment |
| Offer | 4 (Precise Number), 7 (Cost of Time), 8 (Operator Contrast) | 2 (Census) — too diagnostic, not conversion |
| Social Proof | 5 (Identity Shift), 4 (Precise Number), 2 (Census) | 1 (Distinction) — too abstract for proof |

## AI tells — kill on sight

These patterns signal AI generation. They break the voice before the reader finishes the first line.

### Opening tells
- ❌ `Here's the thing:`
- ❌ `Let's talk about`
- ❌ `I want to share something`
- ❌ `Have you ever wondered`
- ❌ `So many creators struggle with`
- ❌ `The truth is,`
- ❌ `Here's what nobody tells you:`
- ✅ Start with the tension, not a setup for the tension

### Enthusiasm tells
- ❌ `I'm so excited to share`
- ❌ `This is a game-changer`
- ❌ `Revolutionary approach`
- ❌ `This changed everything for me`
- ✅ Let the claim create the excitement — never announce it

### Engagement bait tells
- ❌ `What do you think?`
- ❌ `Let me know in the comments`
- ❌ `Change my mind`
- ❌ `Drop a 🙋 if this resonates`
- ✅ Strong closer that doesn't ask for permission

### Structure tells
- ❌ Numbered lists for everything
- ❌ `First... Second... Finally...`
- ❌ `In conclusion`
- ❌ `Here's a breakdown:`
- ✅ Prose with rhythm, or stark parallel structure (the Distinction pattern)

### Velum-specific tells (watch for these in AI output)
- ❌ `unlock your potential`
- ❌ `build the business of your dreams`
- ❌ `you deserve to be paid for your gifts`
- ❌ `it's time to finally`
- ❌ `so many women creators`
- ❌ `I'm passionate about helping`
- ❌ Exclamation marks anywhere in copy
- ✅ See vocabulary system in 00-brand-constitution.md
