## The Velum copy standard

### Voice in one sentence

Calm authority. Every word earns its place. The reader recognises herself — she is not told how to feel.

### The four voice principles

| Principle | In practice |
|---|---|
| Direct without being cold | State the thing. Warmth from specificity, not hedging. Remove qualifiers on sight. |
| Precise without being clinical | Name the actual problem in concrete terms. No abstract framings. |
| Editorial not promotional | No filler, no exclamation marks, no urgency language. A point of view, not a pitch. |
| Authority without ego | Credibility from the system and the document. Never announced, never performed. |

### Banned patterns — rewrite on sight

1. **The negation formula:** "This isn't X. It's not Y. It's Z." Exhausted, reads as AI-generated.
2. Exclamation marks.
3. Emojis in body copy. (Handle watermark `@thevelum.studio` is the only exception.)
4. Urgency tactics: "limited spots", "don't miss out", "act now".
5. Coaching/transformation language: unlock, journey, passion, alignment, abundance, safe space, dream business.
6. Hedging and qualifiers: "just", "maybe", "I think", "kind of", "a little bit".
7. Italics for emphasis in body copy — only the gold italic accent word in headlines.
8. Founder story or personal-brand framing.

### What strong Velum copy looks like

**Strong:**
> "Most creators don't have a business. They have a hobby with a following. The difference is structure — not more content, not better aesthetics, not a rebrand."

**Weak:**
> "I'm so passionate about helping women creators unlock their earning potential and build the business of their dreams. You deserve to be paid for your gifts. 💛"

**Strong:**
> "An audience is attention. A business is structure. Most creators have one. Almost none have built the other."

**Weak:**
> "You've worked so hard to grow your audience — now it's time to finally start making money from it!"

## Copy tasks — how to execute each

### Hook writing

A hook is the ICP's exact situation, stated as fact. No softening, no cleverness for its own sake, no setup.

**Check the hook against:**
- Does it name a tension the ICP already feels? (Not a new idea — a familiar one, finally articulated)
- Is it under 12 words?
- Could it stand alone on a slide with no further context?
- Is it free of banned patterns?

**Patterns to use:**
- The reframe: "[X] isn't the problem. [X-architecture] is."
- The distinction: "[Thing A] is a [category]. [Thing B] is a [business]."
- The census: "[Situation]. [Situation]. [Situation they can't name]."
- The precise number: "[Specific metric] and [gap]."

### Caption writing

```
Line 1: extends the hook — does not repeat it. Adds one new dimension.
Body (2–4 sentences): one argument. One concrete outcome named.
CTA: one, from the approved library. Never stacked.
Footer: — Velum — @thevelum.studio —
Hashtags: max 5, below the footer. Niche-precise. No hashtag walls.
```

### Headline writing (web + carousel)

- Max 6 words for carousel headlines
- One gold italic accent word — the word that shifts the meaning
- Creates tension standalone — would the ICP stop without the next line?
- DM Serif Display — never use this as a font instruction in copy, only as a design direction note

### DM outreach copy

Outreach DMs to ICP prospects identified via Emplifi:

```
Opening: one observation about her content — specific, not flattering
Bridge: name the commercial gap you observed (one sentence)
Signal: soft signal that Velum exists for this — not a pitch
Close: no CTA, no ask — the conversation is the goal
```

**DMs are conversations, not cold emails. Never send a pitch. Never include a link in the opening message.**

### Web copy (hero section)

The hero statement is locked: "You've built a real audience. Nobody taught you how to make a business out of it."

Supporting copy under the hero:
- Names the gap before naming the solution
- No feature list, no service bullets in the hero
- One CTA: the clarity call or the Blueprint DM

## Copy editing checklist

Run every piece of Velum copy through this before returning:

- [ ] No banned vocabulary (check substitution guide in product-marketing.md)
- [ ] No banned patterns (especially the negation formula)
- [ ] No qualifiers or hedging language
- [ ] No exclamation marks
- [ ] Voice: direct, not cold · precise, not clinical · editorial, not promotional
- [ ] One idea per sentence
- [ ] One CTA maximum, named and specific
- [ ] Footer standard present: `— Velum — @thevelum.studio —`
